Addition

Addition is combining numbers together. This is addition:
If I have one candy and you give me 2 candies,
how many candies do I have now? The answer is obviously
3 candies. Here are some super easy addition problems
for you to try out.

  • 1+1
  • 2+4
  • 1+5
  • 7+3
  • 4+9
  • 3+2
  • 5+7
  • 8+1
  • 4+4
  • 7+1
  • 0+4
  • 5+3

Subtraction

Subtraction is taking part of a number away. This is subtraction:
If I have two candies and I give you one candy, how many candies
do I have now? The answer is obviously 1 candy. Here are some
easy subtraction problems for you to do.

  • 1-1
  • 6-2
  • 7-1
  • 3-2
  • 4-4
  • 9-7
  • 8-3
  • 5-2
  • 7-3
  • 8-4
  • 7-6
  • 1-0
  • 3-2

Multiplication

Muliplication is repeating addition. For example, If I have 6 dollars,
and you triple my money, how much money do I now have? The
answer is 18, because 6 tripled is 18. Now, you try your hand at
multplication problems.

  • 9x4
  • 2x3
  • 6x4
  • 1x3
  • 9x0
  • 3x4
  • 6x6
  • 1x1
  • 6x5
  • 2x4
  • 3x8
  • 8x8
  • 1x1
